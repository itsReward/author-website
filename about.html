About Me

My Journey

Born in Lagos, Nigeria, I grew up surrounded by the rich tapestry of stories shared by my grandmother under the baobab tree. These early experiences ignited my passion for storytelling and my deep appreciation for the oral traditions of Africa.

After studying Creative Writing at the University of Cape Town, I embarked on a journey to capture the essence of contemporary African life, blending traditional folklore with modern narratives.

My novels explore themes of identity, migration, family bonds, and the complex interplay between tradition and modernity in African societies. Through my writing, I aim to bridge cultural divides and contribute to the global literary conversation.

When I'm not writing, you can find me teaching creative writing workshops, traveling across the continent gathering stories, or enjoying a cup of hibiscus tea on my veranda while watching the sunset.

Literary Journey

2010

Graduated with honors in Creative Writing from the University of Cape Town.

2012

Published first short story collection, "Whispers of the Baobab," which received critical acclaim.

2015

Established the Lagos Literary Workshop to mentor young African writers.

2018

Received the African Literary Prize for contributions to contemporary African literature.

2021

Launched the "Stories Under Stars" initiative, bringing literature to rural communities across West Africa.

Present

Working on a new novel while continuing to advocate for African literature on the global stage.

My Philosophy

I believe that stories are the threads that weave humanity together. Through my writing, I strive to capture the essence of the human experience while celebrating the rich cultural heritage of Africa.

My goal is to create narratives that resonate across cultural boundaries, inviting readers to explore perspectives they might not otherwise encounter. I see literature as a bridge between worlds, a means to foster understanding and empathy.

I draw inspiration from the everyday lives of people I meet, the landscapes I traverse, and the ancestral wisdom passed down through generations. Each character I create carries a piece of this collective experience, embodying both the universal aspects of humanity and the unique elements of African identity.

Above all, I write to honor the storytellers who came before me and to inspire those who will follow. In a world increasingly divided, stories remind us of our shared humanity and the beauty of our differences.
